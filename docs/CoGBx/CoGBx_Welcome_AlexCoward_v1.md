---

## The very short version

CoGBx sits on top of complex, AI heavy systems.

- It is meant for people who are allergic to tech jargon but still need to steer those systems.
- It uses a small set of metaphors – things like **city**, **studio**, **garden** – so they can say:

> “What this place should feel like and do”

while AIs and developers translate that into flows, code, and guarantees underneath.

In the broader CoCivium ecosystem:

- **CoGBx** is the high level, human facing language (metaphors, sentence shapes, guardrails).
- **CoGbx / CoGibber** are the machine facing structures derived from that language.
- **CoIndex** is the map and registry that keeps track of these languages, portals, and experiments.

This page is your personal on ramp into that corner of the work.

---

## Why I thought this might interest you

ENSO feels like a design spine for the guarantee layer in AI mediated systems. CoGBx is my attempt to give non technical people a way to touch that guarantee layer without ever seeing code or formalism.

If you have any appetite to skim this and react, I would be especially curious about three things:

1. **Metaphors – workable or doomed?**  
   Do the **city / studio / garden** metaphors feel like a sane way for non technical users to talk about “where” they work and learn, or would you expect different metaphor families to work better?

2. **A steward console for nervous newcomers**  
   How might you imagine a **steward console** that lets a nervous newcomer:
   - describe what this place should feel like and do in CoGBx terms, and  
   - set a few guardrails (“never do X without asking”, “always show Y when Z happens”),
   without needing to read specs or see anything that looks like code?

3. **Research and business angles**  
   Do you see interesting angles around:
   - **pattern libraries** (reusable CoGBx patterns for common situations),
   - **literacy paths** (how people become comfortable with the language), or
   - **steward consoles** as products that sit between “people who do not speak tech” and the AI systems that act on their behalf?

Separately, I am starting to use the same ideas in a small project called **GroupBuild**. It is essentially a field lab where people whose work is being reshaped by AI get together to “reinvent work”. CoGBx is my attempt to turn the way they naturally talk about these places into something AIs can safely act on.
