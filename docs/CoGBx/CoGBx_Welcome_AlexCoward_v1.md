---

## The very short version

CoGBx sits on top of complex, AI heavy systems.

- It is meant for people who are allergic to tech jargon but still need to steer those systems.
- It uses a small set of metaphors - things like **city**, **studio**, **garden** - so they can say:

> "What this place should feel like and do"

while AIs and developers translate that into flows, code, and guarantees underneath.

In the broader CoCivium ecosystem:

- **CoGBx** is the high level, human facing language (metaphors, sentence shapes, guardrails).
- **CoGbx / CoGibber** are the machine facing structures derived from that language.
- **CoIndex** is the map and registry that keeps track of these languages, portals, and experiments.

This page is your personal on ramp into that corner of the work.

## Why we thought this might interest you

ENSO feels like a design spine for the guarantee layer in AI mediated systems. CoGBx is our attempt to give non technical people a way to touch that guarantee layer without ever seeing code or formalism.

Alongside CoGBx, we are publishing a "CoTheory of Civilization" - a suite of pieces aimed at academics and strategists that tries to make explicit what kinds of "stable states" a human plus AI civilization can fall into, and how our tools push us toward one or another.

Very short version of that theory:

- Civilizations tend to settle into a small number of attractors, such as:
  - **core controlled stability** (power and tools concentrated in a small core), and
  - **edge balanced stability** (power and tools pushed closer to the edge where people actually live).
- In past eras, the most capable tools were expensive and slow to spread, so elite cores could be dangerous but not all powerful.
- With modern tools - especially AI, networks, and automation - a small core could, in principle, drive the system toward the least desired stability: a cruel global dynasty that uses AI to lock in advantage and suppress edge voices for a very long time.

CoCivium exists to pull in the opposite direction: toward edge balanced futures where the tools of civilization are understandable and steerable at the edge, not just inside cores. CoGBx is one of the ways we are trying to make that practical.

Our own instinct is to play a back office, steward style role rather than chase crowns or limelight. We would be happiest if the ideas and tools outlive any of our names. If at any point it felt interesting to you to help shape or even co author parts of this CoTheory of Civilization work, we would welcome that.

If you have any appetite to skim this and react, we would be especially curious about three things:

1. **Metaphors - workable or doomed?**  
   Do the **city / studio / garden** metaphors feel like a sane way for non technical users to talk about "where" they work and learn, or would you expect different metaphor families to work better?

2. **A steward console for nervous newcomers**  
   How might you imagine a **steward console** that lets a nervous newcomer:
   - describe what this place should feel like and do in CoGBx terms, and  
   - set a few guardrails ("never do X without asking", "always show Y when Z happens"),
   without needing to read specs or see anything that looks like code?

3. **Research and business angles**  
   Do you see interesting angles around:
   - **pattern libraries** (reusable CoGBx patterns for common situations),
   - **literacy paths** (how people become comfortable with the language), or
   - **steward consoles** as products that sit between "people who do not speak tech" and the AI systems that act on their behalf?

Separately, we are starting to use the same ideas in a small project called **GroupBuild**. It is essentially a field lab where people whose work is being reshaped by AI get together to "reinvent work". CoGBx is our attempt to turn the way they naturally talk about these places into something AIs can safely act on.
