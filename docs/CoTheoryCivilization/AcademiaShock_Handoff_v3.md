## 3. Core theory snapshot (for quick reuse)

**CTC premise:**  
AI is now a **civilizational infrastructure**, not just a productivity tool.  
The question is:

> What kinds of civilization do AI-augmented structures make easier,  
> and which futures do they quietly suppress?

### 3.1 Five overlapping phases

1. **AI Containerization**  
   - AI as “helper” inside proprietary containers.  
   - Workflows and data migrate into closed ecosystems.

2. **AI Platform Wars**  
   - Race to own chips → models → infra → apps.  
   - Ecosystems become API-dependent “vassals”.

3. **AI Power Wars**  
   - AI treated as sovereign strategic asset (defense, intel, economy).  
   - Frontier capabilities diverge from public models.

4. **Elite Convergence & AI Cartels**  
   - Coordination around “safety” and standards mostly by incumbents.  
   - De-facto AI cartels around compute, data, and frontier models.

5. **Civilizational Fork**  
   - As AI saturates infrastructure, civilization drifts toward one of two attractors.

### 3.2 Two attractors

**1) Authoritarian Convergence — “Empire of Automation”**

- Centralized AI stack tightly coupled to states and major corporations.
- Continuous scoring, surveillance, and personalized persuasion.
- Corruption in **code and configuration**, not envelopes.
- Citizens live in **personalized reality tunnels**.
- Self-authorship erodes: “What does the system expect of me?”

**2) Edge-Governed Pluralism — “Chorus of Many Edges”**

- **Edges** (cities, communities, orgs) co-govern AI systems.
- Infrastructures are **forkable, auditable, contestable**.
- **Truth meshes**: multiple sources, mapped disagreements, explicit uncertainty.
- **Steward Circles** sit between AI, law, and community.
- Democracy becomes a continuous, AI-assisted deliberation process.
