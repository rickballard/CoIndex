This segment introduces the idea that human plus AI civilizations drift toward
a small set of attractors.

It sets up a contrast between:

- core controlled futures
  - where a small number of cores (institutions, platforms, regimes, stacked
    infrastructures) can lock in advantage,
  - and where AI plus automation makes it cheap to surveil, steer, and
    punish at global scale.

- edge balanced futures
  - where tools and guarantees keep understanding and steering power closer to
    the edge,
  - where many small groups can still invent, veto, and re-route.

It also names the least desired attractor:

- the cruel dynasty
  - a long lived pattern where a global core treats humans as resources to be
    managed,
  - and where even the sycophants who helped build that core are eventually
    eaten by it.
