What is CoGBx in one breath?

CoGBx is a simple way to say what you want AI systems to do — and not do — using short, reusable sentence shapes. Think of it as "guardrail talk": you describe how you want to be protected, what you want to see, and when you want to be asked.

You don't have to memorise anything. The system will suggest sentences and you can tweak them in your own words.

Plain language
"If the news feed keeps showing the same couple of websites, warn me. I want variety."
CoGBx-style guardrail
WHEN my news feed collapses to a few sources,
ALWAYS show me a warning and more diverse options.

Six useful sentence shapes

Most people only ever need a handful of CoGBx patterns:

  • WHEN / ALWAYS – "When X happens, always show me Y."
  • NEVER WITHOUT ASKING – "Never do X without asking me first."
  • LIMIT – "Limit X to Y for Z."
  • ALERT – "If X crosses Y, send me an alert."
  • EXPLAIN – "Explain in simple language why X happened."
  • PROTECT – "Protect me from X, even if it would make more money."

Behind the scenes, each of these patterns is turned into a more detailed machine rule. But you never have to see that unless you want to.
